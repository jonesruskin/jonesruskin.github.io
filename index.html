OPC Cement vs PPC Cement: A Real rival tale

Read in 5 min

Prasanna Venkkatesh

It is not uncommon that people have doubts and confusions when it comes to choosing the type of cement that is to be used in their homes. Although there are numerous types of cement the one pair which has a regular head on clash is OPC and PPC, especially in residential constructions. OPC stands for Ordinary Portland Cement & PPC stands for Portland Pozzolana Cement. To know more about the rivals we must take a look back at the history, a minute.

Ordinary Portland Cement was first introduced in the mid 19th century by Joseph Aspdin of  England and named it Portland cement, because the render made from it was in color similar to then prestigious Portland stone quarried on the  Portland, England. However, Aspdins' cement was nothing like modern Portland cement but was a first step in its development, called a proto-Portland cement. Over period of time it is found by practice that when OPC is added with other Pozzolonic materials like fly ash, volcanic ash & rice husk ash its character and strength gaining capacity remained unaltered at the end. So, PPC is an upgraded form of OPC.

The Grades

Usually OPC comes in garde: 33,43,53. In which the numbers represent the strength the cement mortar will achieve on the 28th day in N/mm2. When it comes to ppc, according to the IS codal provisions it is said to be of a grade 33. However leading cement manufacturers claim that their PPC cement tends to attain the compressive strength of 53 on its 28 th day (grade 53). Hmmm, needs to be tested.

Setting (Curing time) time

As said earlier, different grades of cement gain their respective strengths on their 28th day. However the initial setting time is what sets apart PPC from OPC. While OPC tends to achieve strength much quicker and set quickly, the PPC tends to set gradually but within the 28 th day.

For example, In practice, if OPC is used for construction then it emits large amount of heat (due to heat of hydration during curing) for a short period of time & its curing time /DESHUTTERING is not more than, 7 days. And PPC emits small amount of heat (low rate of heat of hydration) but over long period & its curing time is 28 days theoretically, however in practice it is only done for 15-21 for DESHUTTERING days. Although it is not a wrong practice, extreme care should be taken while curing, irrespective of the type and grades.

Tne Native

For ordinary purposes OPC is desired and when it comes to mass concrete like dams, PPC is strictly advised since it has low setting time and low rate of heat of hydration. In residential homes, OPC is suitable for columns, beams, slabs which require quick setting time to be economical. PPC has an edge on OPC as PPC has slower rate of heat of hydration. Since PPC is prone to less cracks & reduced shrinkage, better workability and finishing it is recommended for brick masonry, plastering, tiling and waterproofing works, where, strength is not the major criteria.

The market king

Although OPC & PPC are almost same in the view of engineers, PPC have gained attention lately. The reason is that OPC tends to emit more amount of Carbon-dioxide into the environment during its reaction with water during concreting and thus affecting the environment. On the other side PPC surpasses OPC by using the environment wastes such as fly ash, etc for manufacturing thus gaining love among green lovers. A direct hit!

Also, since PPC tends to be cheaper than OPC, it now rules the market, a almost monopoly. And seeing the trend the manufacturers also have moved towards PPC and have stopped OPC’s preproduction and supply. However the desire of OPC doesn’t fade away with certain mass of people and some people are still betting on OPC. If you are one among them then you can order it in/with cement manufacturing companies with their minimum order quantity, at least 100 bags.

Bottomline

Although both cements are same technically. On the Nutshell when it comes to residential constructions PPC is desired as it is cheaper and has minimum chances of developing cracks ( since curing and setting period is long) and also provides much better resistance in aggressive climate. And OPC still on the basket, for places which strictly requires quick setting.

Note: Both OPC and PPC can be a substitute to each other but only with better quality control and by adopting best construction practices. Adopt an economical option while choosing which type of cement has to be used for house building.

So what would you choose?
