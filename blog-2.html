Load Bearing Structures vs Framed Structures

Load bearing structures are in practice from ancient days and the now evolved form of structures are what we call as Framed Structures. Framed structures are the buildings that you mostly come by every day, today.

The load bearing structures & framed structures are mainly differentiated by their load transferring mechanism. That is how the load (Weight) of the building is transferred to the foundation and then from foundation to the earth.

In load bearing structure wall is built over the basement(/foundation) and slab(Roof) is placed over it. Here the load is transferred directly from slab to wall, thus making the brickwalls to bear the load, thus named as load bearing structures.

Framed Structures consists of the structural elements like Beam, Column (Pillar). In Framed Structures the load is transferred in 4 stages, initially from slab (Roof slab) to Beam and then from beam to Column (what we call as pillar) and then from column to the foundation, then over to the earth.

Here the main difference is that in case of Load Bearing structures, walls transfers the load and in case of the framed structures, walls only act as Partition between rooms and don’t carry any load (weight from above).

Load bearing structures are cheaper while framed structurers are comparatively of higher cost. However the Load Bearing structures are not suitable for all types of constructions. Whereas, Framed structures can be constructed in any condition.

The main things that are to be considered while choosing between Load Bearing Structures and the Framed Structures are, the Size (area) of the building, the number of floors to be built, and the Floor plan differences among the other floors.

Load Bearing Structures

Opting to Load Bearing structure is wise,

In case the building that is to be built is not more than 1500 Sq. ft., provided the building that is to be built is either of one Floor (Ground Floor) or two floors ( G+1) only. i.e. load bearing structures are suitable for structures where only Ground (G) Floor or Ground Floor + First Floor(G+1) is to be built and are not suitable in structures, where more than two floors (ex: G+2) are desired both initially as well as/ or for future extensions.

One other major factor that is to be considered while choosing Load Bearing Structure is that both the floors (Ground Floor & First Floor) must be of same Floor plans, where wall positions doesn’t differ.

Framed Structures

But Framed Structures must be strictly implemented,

  • In case if the area of the building is more than 1500 Sq. ft. or if the rooms are of larger spans (since Roof Slabs are larger).
  • If the building has more than two floors ( i.e., G+2, G+3,….so on) or in case if the building has only Ground Floor and more than one floor( > G+1) are further desired/to be built above in future.
  • If the building does not have same floor plan among different floors (like changes in wall position, Orientations, etc.).

One of the common mistake that we come across easily while choosing between Load Bearing Structures & Framed Structures is that Load bearing structures are implemented even for buildings having more than two floors (say G+2), such practices may appear to be a wise choice economically, but only for a short period of time. In such cases the buildings may get structurally weak thereby reducing the overall life of the structure.

If you want to/ People tend to build Ground floor and then later construct First floor for renting purposes of smaller partitions where the Floor plans won’t be identical and wall positions vary. In such cases Framed structures should be strictly implemented.

Conclusion

Although Load bearing structures are economical, they are not ideal in all conditions. Both Load Bearing Structures and Framed Structures are suitable & has an edge over each other depending upon the type of building that you desire & intend to build. Basically, the 3 parameters – Area, Number of floors & Floor plan are enough to choose between Load bearing structure and framed structure. So, make sure they are answered.
